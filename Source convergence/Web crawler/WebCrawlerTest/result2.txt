										<p>Traditionally, <b>physical health</b> has been defined as the absence of disease or serious illness. However, in recent years, modern medicine has changed this viewpoint. The <a class="external" href="https://study.com/learn/lesson/world-health-organization-history-purpose.html">World Health Organization</a> has expanded the definition of health beyond the scope of sickness and disease. The WHO now defines <b>health </b>in the sense of the overall well-being of a person, physically, mentally, and socially. These aspects are intricately linked, and the health of an individual encompasses all of them.</p>
										<div class="hidden" ng-non-bindable><p>Physical and mental health have a very close connection. Poor mental health puts a person at a greater risk of <a class="external" href="https://study.com/learn/lesson/basic-medical-terms-acute-chronic-diseases.html">chronic</a> physical conditions. Additionally, chronic physical conditions are linked with an increased risk of poor mental health. This connection is not only seen between physical and mental health. Physical health is linked to all of the other dimensions of health. If a person's physical health declines, this often leads to a decline in the other areas. For example, if a person is battling a serious illness, this can lead to a decline in social well-being. The person may avoid others because of contagiousness or simply because of a decreased desire to be around others. This, in turn, can lead to sadness, which is a decline in mental and <a class="external" href="https://study.com/learn/lesson/psychological-health-emotional-mental-examples.html">emotional health</a>.</p></div>

	<meta itemprop="transcript" content="Defining Physical Health Traditional definitions of physical health prior to the onset of modern medicine would have considered someone physically healthy if he or she was not stricken with a serious illness. With modern medical innovations came longer life spans, which changed the way we define physical health. Today's definition can consider everything ranging from the absence of disease to fitness level. While physical health consists of many components, here is a brief list of the key areas that should be addressed: Physical activity - includes strength, flexibility, and endurance Nutrition and diet - includes nutrient intake, fluid intake, and healthy digestion Alcohol and drugs - includes the abstinence from or reduced consumption of these substances Medical self-care - includes addressing minor ailments or injuries and seeking emergency care as necessary Rest and sleep - includes periodic rest and relaxation, along with high quality sleep Components of Physical Health Below are ways that each key area of physical health can be addressed through lifestyle choices: Physical activity: Most healthy children and adults should be active on a daily basis. This should be a mix of both leisurely physical activity and structured exercise. Examples of leisurely physical activity include hiking, biking, and walking. Examples of more structured forms of exercise include strength training, running, and sports. Nutrition and diet: A well-balanced diet should contain carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals. Restricting specific nutrients should only be done under the supervision of a licensed health professional. Fluid, ideally in the form of clean water, should be regularly consumed. Meals and snacks should be consumed throughout the day, and portion sizes should be sensible. Alcohol and drugs: Substances that alter mood or other bodily processes should be limited or avoided. Those with addictive tendencies or other health risks should consider complete abstinence from these substances. Medical self-care: Basic items, such as bandages, lozenges, and over-the-counter pain-relieving medications, should be easily accessible from home. Long-term coughing, fevers, or other ailments should be addressed through primary care. Emergency treatment should be sought when signs and symptoms are significant or life-threatening. Rest and sleep: While regular activity is essential for physical health, allowing the body to rest is just as important. Spending time relaxing or taking short naps can help rejuvenate the body. Sleep should take place in a quiet, dark environment and should last approximately 7-9 hours. Consistent sleep that is much shorter or longer than this duration, or is low quality, may need to be addressed by a health professional. Physical Health Assessments If you have visited a physician or personal trainer recently, you might know that assessing physical health can be done in a variety of ways. The following measurements can be used to test certain aspects of physical health: General assessments - includes weight, body mass index (BMI), and reflex tests. Disease risk factor - assessments includes blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood glucose tests. Fitness assessments - includes body composition (body fat percentage), flexibility, muscular strength, and endurance tests Here are some simple, yet effective, examples of the various ways you can assess your own physical health: Heart rate: This can be taken by pressing lightly on the underside of the wrist on the thumb side. Count the number of beats you feel in 15 seconds and multiply by four. The average adult has a heart rate between 60 and 100. Lower heart rates are typically the result of advanced levels of physical conditioning. Heart rates outside of this range, especially when accompanied by fatigue, shortness of breath, or dizziness, may be signs of a more significant problem. Body temperature: While 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit is the typically-recognized normal temperature, healthy body temperatures can vary slightly. A temperature above 100.4 degrees Fahrenheit signals a fever caused by an infection or illness. Temperatures below normal may result from cold exposure, alcohol or drug use, or hormonal abnormalities. Grip strength: If you struggle to open jars or hang on to objects for more than a few minutes, this could be a sign that overall muscle strength is low. For those seeking for a more exact measurement of grip strength, many hospitals, clinics, and health clubs have instruments called dynamometers that provide more specific measurements. Toe touch: Simply bending at the waist and reaching for your toes can be a great test of your flexibility and physical health. The further you are from reaching your toes, the more likely it is that your physical health is poor. Lesson Summary Physical health can be determined by many things, but some major components can include physical activity, nutrition and diet, alcohol and drugs, medical self-care, and rest and sleep. Healthy physical activity can be determined by leisurely physical activity, like hiking, or structured exercise, like playing sports. Nutrition and diet in a healthy person is determined by a balanced diet, water consumption, and sensible portions. Either a limited use of or abstinence from alcohol and drugs can also lead to greater physical health. Medical self-care can be determined both by small efforts, like using bandages and lozenges, or larger steps, like knowing when to go to the hospital. Rest and sleep are also important for physical health, and a healthy person should get 7-9 hours of sleep each night, and may also take naps. Some ways to self-monitor physical health can include monitoring heart rate, body temperature, grip strength, and toe touching. Learning Outcomes When you are finished with this lesson, you should be able to: State what physical health entails List and describe the key areas of physical health Recall ways to assess your physical health"/>

Going to bed at the same time each night, maintaining a quiet and dark environment, and avoiding blue light and caffeine before bedtime are all ways to promote restful sleep. Quality sleep is important. A lack of sleep can lead to an increased risk of chronic diseases and conditions. These include: type 2 diabetes, heart disease, obesity, and depression.</p><p>The amount of required sleep varies by age. Preschool age children need 10-13 hours of sleep each night. School age children need 9-12 hours of sleep each night. Teenagers need 8-10 hours of sleep nightly, while adults need at least 7 hours of good sleep. A person who consistently gets fewer than five hours of sleep per night should be evaluated by a health professional to determine if an underlying issue is causing this.</p><table border="0" class="imageplugin">

</table><p></p><h3 id="section---EatingWell">Eating Well</h3><p>Good <b>nutrition</b> is another key aspect of physical health. Good nutrition includes eating a variety of <a class="external" href="https://study.com/academy/lesson/food-overview-types-importance.html">foods</a>. Nutritious foods that are high in vitamins and minerals include vegetables, fruits, whole grains, beans, nuts, seeds, lean meat, seafood, eggs, and low fat dairy products. It is important to avoid foods that are high in sugar, saturated fats, and sodium. Making good nutritional choices can promote longer life, lower the risk of serious disease, and help to manage chronic conditions like heart disease and diabetes.</p><p>A person who is looking to improve their daily diet should consider adding fiber, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates. Reducing sugar, salt, and alcohol are also important steps to take. Finally, eating regular, proportional meals is important. Skipping meals or eating too many large meals can disrupt metabolism. This, in turn, can lead to decreased physical and mental health.</p><p>It is also important to drink enough water each day. Drinking water prevents dehydration. It also helps to prevent conditions such as kidney stones, constipation, and hypertension. It keeps the body working well, aids mood and cognitive function, and can help a person manage arthritis.</p>
										<div class="hidden" ng-non-bindable><h3 id="section---PhysicalActivity">Physical Activity</h3><p>Physical health involves regular <b><a class="external" href="https://study.com/academy/lesson/physical-activity-health-fitness-and-performance.html">physical activity</a></b>. A physically healthy person develops exercise routines that can be maintained. These should be activities that are enjoyable and that can be woven into the daily routine of life. These can include participation in a structured sport such as tennis or golf. It may also include more leisurely activities such as walks, bike rides, or trips to the gym. If the activity is enjoyable and accessible, it is more likely to be repeated regularly. Regular repetition leads to increased physical health.</p><p>Regular activity not only has an impact on the body, but it also has an impact on the brain. Regular physical activity can lower anxiety. It can improve thinking and judgment skills. It can even lead to improved sleep quality for people.</p><p>Additionally, regular physical activity can reduce the risk of developing disease. One hundred fifty minutes of activity per week lowers the risk of cardiovascular conditions like heart disease and stroke. It can also lower the risk of conditions such as type 2 diabetes and even some cancers. Even if a person is already suffering from a chronic condition, regular physical activity can help to maintain the condition. Regular physical activity helps a person to maintain a healthy weight, strengthen bones, and strengthen muscles. All of these things can lead to improved health.</p><h3 id="section---Hygiene">Hygiene</h3><p><b><a class="external" href="https://study.com/learn/lesson/personal-hygiene-importance-practices.html">Hygiene</a></b> is another important component of physical health. Hygiene involves any actions that help a person to maintain health and prevent disease. These may include daily practices such as showering, washing hands, brushing teeth, and flossing. They may also include long-term practices such as finding accessible medical help and accessing preventative care. Preventative care includes activities like having a yearly physical checkup, engaging in regular dental visits, and seeing the eye doctor regularly.</p><table border="0" class="imageplugin">

Mentally, good hygiene makes a person feel good, which in turn raises self-confidence. Socially, a confident person is better able to connect with others.</p><h3 id="section---Relaxation">Relaxation</h3><p>Another key aspect of physical health is <b>relaxation</b>. It is important that a person take time to enjoy activities and step back from the stresses of life. If a person does not make time for relaxation, it can lead to a variety of physical problems. Headaches, muscle tension, and back pain can be a result of a lack of relaxation.</p><p>Constant stress can lead to the overproduction of stress hormones as well. A theory related to this is that of adrenal fatigue. Proponents of this theory believe that chronic stress causes the body to be in a constant state of fight or flight. This causes the adrenal glands to secrete hormones such as cortisol. When the body is in this state regularly, the adrenal glands can't keep up with the demand for the stress hormone, and adrenal fatigue is the result. Adrenal fatigue can lead to tiredness, insomnia, and cravings.</p></div>

Some of these assessments may be completed during an annual physical exam. Others can be completed regularly at home.</p><h4 id="section---GeneralHealthAssessments">General Health Assessments</h4><ul> 
 <li><b>BMI</b>: This stands for body mass index. It is a measure of a person's weight in relation to their height. Adults should aim for a BMI of less than 25.</li> 
 <li><b>Reflexes</b>: These are neurological tests that are often completed as part of an annual exam. A doctor checks reflexes to make sure signals and messages are being transmitted and interpreted correctly by the nervous system.</li> 
</ul><h4 id="section---DiseaseRiskFactorAssessments">Disease Risk Factor Assessments</h4><p>Doctors will typically run routine tests as part of an annual exam. These may include:</p>

 <li><b>Blood Pressure Checks</b></li> 
 <li><b>Cholesterol Checks</b>: Cholesterol checks are typically performed every few years. They are important in assessing the risk of heart attack and stroke.</li> 
</ul><h4 id="section---FitnessAssessments">Fitness Assessments</h4><p>These can be administered by a doctor or done at home. They include:</p><ul> 
 <li><b>Flexibility Tests</b>: these involve stretching exercises</li> 
 <li><b>Endurance Tests</b>: these assess cardiovascular function.</li> 
</ul><h4 id="section---SelfAssessments">Self-Assessments</h4><p>Several other self-assessments include measuring heart rate, taking body temperature readings, and testing grip strength. A typical resting heart rate is between 60 and 100 beats per minute. A typical body temperature is around 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit.</p><p>All of these assessments can give a measure of the physical health of a person. They can be used to evaluate current physical fitness as well as assess risk of future disease.</p></div>

The modern definition of health includes more than just the absence of disease or sickness. <b>Health</b> now includes an overall sense of well-being. Physical, mental, and social wellness are now all key factors of health. Each of these areas is intricately linked with the others as well.</p><p>There are several types of activities that promote physical health.</p><ul> 
 <li><b>Aerobic Activities</b>: Activities promoting the use of large muscles groups, including running or biking</li> 
 <li><b>Muscle-strengthening Activities</b>: Activities such as weight training, pushups, or sit ups that have the muscles work against an applied force</li> 
 <li><b>Bone-strengthening Activities</b>: Activities that cause in impact with the ground, such as running or jumping rope</li> 
 <li><b>Stretching Activities:</b> Activities such as yoga which promote flexibility and increased movement</li>

This diet should include vitamins, minerals, healthy fats, lean protein, and complex carbohydrates. Regular exercise routines and regular health screenings are also important aspects of physical health. Consistent, quality sleep of at least seven hours per night for adults is key to good physical health. Finally, good hygiene helps to keep a person healthy.</p><p>A physically healthy person will not only work to make healthy choices in the present, but they will also work to prevent future ailments and disease. This can be done through the use of regular health assessments which include:</p><ul> 
 <li><b>General health assessments</b> such as a BMI assessment and reflex tests</li> 
 <li><b>Disease risk factor assessments</b> such as blood pressure and cholesterol monitoring to predict risk of future cardiovascular disease.</li> 
 <li><b>Fitness assessments </b>such as flexibility and endurance testing to give an overall fitness rating</li> 
 <li><b>Self-assessments</b> such as heart rate monitoring (60-100 beats per minute at rest), body temperature readings (98.6 degrees Fahrenheit), and grip strength tests</li> 
</ul><p>Attention to physical health is important for everyone. It relates to many other aspects of wellness, it can decrease risk for many diseases, and it can help a person to live a longer, healthier life.</p></div>

															<h2 id="section---DefiningPhysicalHealth">Defining Physical Health</h2><p>Traditional definitions of <b>physical health</b> prior to the onset of modern medicine would have considered someone physically healthy if he or she was not stricken with a serious illness. With modern medical innovations came longer life spans, which changed the way we define physical health. Today's definition can consider everything ranging from the absence of disease to fitness level. </p><p>While physical health consists of many components, here is a brief list of the key areas that should be addressed: </p><ul> 
 <li>Physical activity - includes strength, flexibility, and endurance </li> 
 <li>Nutrition and diet - includes nutrient intake, fluid intake, and healthy digestion </li> 
 <li>Alcohol and drugs - includes the abstinence from or reduced consumption of these substances </li> 
 <li>Medical self-care - includes addressing minor ailments or injuries and seeking emergency care as necessary </li> 
 <li>Rest and sleep - includes periodic rest and relaxation, along with high quality sleep </li> 
</ul>
															<h2 id="section---ComponentsOfPhysicalHealth">Components of Physical Health</h2><p>Below are ways that each key area of physical health can be addressed through lifestyle choices: </p><p>Physical activity: Most healthy children and adults should be active on a daily basis. This should be a mix of both leisurely physical activity and structured exercise. Examples of leisurely physical activity include hiking, biking, and walking. Examples of more structured forms of exercise include strength training, running, and sports. </p><p>Nutrition and diet: A well-balanced diet should contain carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals. Restricting specific nutrients should only be done under the supervision of a licensed health professional. Fluid, ideally in the form of clean water, should be regularly consumed. Meals and snacks should be consumed throughout the day, and portion sizes should be sensible. </p><p>Alcohol and drugs: Substances that alter mood or other bodily processes should be limited or avoided. Those with addictive tendencies or other health risks should consider complete abstinence from these substances. </p><p>Medical self-care: Basic items, such as bandages, lozenges, and over-the-counter pain-relieving medications, should be easily accessible from home. Long-term coughing, fevers, or other ailments should be addressed through primary care. Emergency treatment should be sought when signs and symptoms are significant or life-threatening. </p><p>Rest and sleep: While regular activity is essential for physical health, allowing the body to rest is just as important. Spending time relaxing or taking short naps can help rejuvenate the body. Sleep should take place in a quiet, dark environment and should last approximately 7-9 hours. Consistent sleep that is much shorter or longer than this duration, or is low quality, may need to be addressed by a health professional. </p><h2 id="section---PhysicalHealthAssessments">Physical Health Assessments</h2><p>If you have visited a physician or personal trainer recently, you might know that assessing physical health can be done in a variety of ways. The following measurements can be used to test certain aspects of physical health: </p><ul> 
 <li>General assessments - includes weight, body mass index (BMI), and reflex tests. </li> 
 <li>Disease risk factor - assessments includes blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood glucose tests. </li> 
 <li>Fitness assessments - includes body composition (body fat percentage), flexibility, muscular strength, and endurance tests </li> 
</ul><p>Here are some simple, yet effective, examples of the various ways you can assess your own physical health: </p>
													</div>
												</div>
												<div class="hidden" ng-non-bindable><p>Heart rate: This can be taken by pressing lightly on the underside of the wrist on the thumb side. Count the number of beats you feel in 15 seconds and multiply by four. The average adult has a heart rate between 60 and 100. Lower heart rates are typically the result of advanced levels of physical conditioning. Heart rates outside of this range, especially when accompanied by fatigue, shortness of breath, or dizziness, may be signs of a more significant problem. </p><p>Body temperature: While 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit is the typically-recognized normal temperature, healthy body temperatures can vary slightly. A temperature above 100.4 degrees Fahrenheit signals a fever caused by an infection or illness. Temperatures below normal may result from cold exposure, alcohol or drug use, or hormonal abnormalities. </p><p>Grip strength: If you struggle to open jars or hang on to objects for more than a few minutes, this could be a sign that overall muscle strength is low. For those seeking for a more exact measurement of grip strength, many hospitals, clinics, and health clubs have instruments called dynamometers that provide more specific measurements. </p><p>Toe touch: Simply bending at the waist and reaching for your toes can be a great test of your flexibility and physical health. The further you are from reaching your toes, the more likely it is that your physical health is poor. </p><h2 id="section---LessonSummary">Lesson Summary</h2><p><b>Physical health</b> can be determined by many things, but some major components can include physical activity, nutrition and diet, alcohol and drugs, medical self-care, and rest and sleep. </p><p>Healthy physical activity can be determined by leisurely physical activity, like hiking, or structured exercise, like playing sports. Nutrition and diet in a healthy person is determined by a balanced diet, water consumption, and sensible portions. Either a limited use of or abstinence from alcohol and drugs can also lead to greater physical health. Medical self-care can be determined both by small efforts, like using bandages and lozenges, or larger steps, like knowing when to go to the hospital. Rest and sleep are also important for physical health, and a healthy person should get 7-9 hours of sleep each night, and may also take naps. </p><p>Some ways to self-monitor physical health can include monitoring heart rate, body temperature, grip strength, and toe touching. </p><h2 id="section---LearningOutcomes">Learning Outcomes</h2><p>When you are finished with this lesson, you should be able to: </p><ul>
 <li>State what physical health entails </li>
 <li>List and describe the key areas of physical health </li>
 <li>Recall ways to assess your physical health</li>
</ul></div>
